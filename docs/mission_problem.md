# Mission, Problem Statement & Hypothesis — UnseenFutures (Riseup Africa)

## Mission
Empower African youth who have graduated from high school or hold a university degree through verified access to donors and opportunities, with priority given to:
- Refugee youth
- Internally Displaced Persons (IDPs)
- Vulnerable youth
- Persons with Disabilities (PWDs)

Verification mechanisms:
- Letters of recommendation from camp, IDP settlement, or community chairmen
- Field verification by the platform team to authenticate before youth details are forwarded to donors

## Problem Statement
Many African graduates — especially marginalized youth — lack access to verified post-secondary opportunities, while donors have no reliable way to identify eligible beneficiaries.

What: Absence of verified platforms linking post-high school youth to opportunities; verification is weak or nonexistent.
When: Post-high school graduation — for scholarships, internships, mentorships, and vocational training.
Where: Camps, rural, and urban poor neighborhoods.
Why: Youth are invisible, and donors cannot identify genuine beneficiaries.
How: Platform integrates document + recommendation letter verification and field verification, bringing transparency, equity, and efficiency.

## Hypothesis
- Confirmed youth profiles (documents + recommendation letters + field verification) increase access to post-secondary opportunities.
- Donors/NGOs will identify beneficiaries with greater certainty, enhancing transparency.
- Youth receive structured, actionable access to opportunities they qualify for.
